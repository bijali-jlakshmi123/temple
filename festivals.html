Festivals at Sree Lakshmana Swami Temple

The Sree Lakshmana Swami Temple is known for its vibrant and spiritually uplifting festivals that attract devotees from across the region. These celebrations are deeply rooted in tradition and bring together people in devotion and joy.

Lakshmana Jayanthi

The temple's most significant festival, *Lakshmana Jayanthi*, commemorates the birth of Lord Lakshmana. The day is marked with special poojas, *Abhishekam* (ritual bathing), cultural programs, and a grand procession of the deity.

Navaratri

The nine-day Navaratri festival is a time of intense devotion and celebration. Each day is dedicated to different forms of the divine, culminating in a grand ceremony on Vijayadashami.

Vishu

Vishu, the Malayalam New Year, is celebrated with great enthusiasm at the temple. Devotees gather for the *Vishu Kani* and seek blessings for prosperity and happiness in the coming year.

Annual Ratholsavam (Chariot Festival)

The *Ratholsavam*, or chariot festival, is a spectacular event where the deity is taken in a grand procession on a beautifully decorated chariot. The festival signifies unity and collective devotion.

Annadanam (Food Offering)

During major festivals, the temple organizes *Annadanam*, a community meal offered to all devotees. It reflects the spirit of sharing and gratitude.
